## A world of insects, steam, and strange civilizations

**Insect Steampunk** is a pen-and-paper roleplaying game set in a world where insectoid societies, strange technologies, and unfamiliar cultures shape everyday life.

It is a project born from a long fascination with tabletop RPGs, worldbuilding, and the idea that fantasy can be far more imaginative than the familiar medieval clichés it is so often reduced to.

At its heart, Insect Steampunk is an attempt to build a world that feels both alien and believable — a world of colonies, castes, machinery, rituals, conflict, survival, and discovery.

---

## What kind of game is it?

Insect Steampunk is a **tabletop roleplaying game** in the broad tradition of games like **Dungeons & Dragons**, but with its own setting, tone, and design direction.

Players enter a world inspired by:

- insect societies and behavior
- industrial and steampunk aesthetics
- unusual cultures and social systems
- strange environments and survival pressures
- fantasy that feels unfamiliar rather than conventional

The goal is not only to offer another RPG system, but to create a setting that invites players to think differently about what a fantasy world can be.

---

## Why this world exists

The project grew out of a long-term interest in fantasy, games, and mythic imagination.

Many fantasy worlds today still draw from the same narrow pool of imagery and assumptions. Insect Steampunk began as an attempt to move beyond that by asking a different question:

**What if fantasy were built from a different foundation altogether?**

Instead of castles, elves, and familiar heroic tropes, this world explores:

- hive-like societies
- instinct and intelligence
- strange technologies
- survival under pressure
- forms of civilization that feel less human, yet still deeply understandable

The result is a setting designed to feel imaginative, internally coherent, and distinct.

---

## What makes the rules different?

Insect Steampunk uses a custom tabletop RPG system developed specifically for this setting. Rather than treating character progression as a simple increase in numbers, the rules are built around structured growth, meaningful specialization, and a more distinctive sense of development.

The goal is not to take a generic fantasy ruleset and place it into a different world. Instead, the system and the setting are being developed together, so that mechanics, atmosphere, and character options support the same vision.

This should create a roleplaying experience that feels different not only because of the world itself, but also because of how characters evolve within it.

## Design direction

The design philosophy behind Insect Steampunk is guided by a few key ideas.

### A world first

The setting is not just a backdrop for mechanics. The world itself is one of the main reasons to play.

### Strange, but meaningful

The goal is not randomness or absurdity, but a world that feels unfamiliar in a way that still makes sense.

### Fantasy beyond habit

The project tries to move away from the idea that fantasy must always resemble the same recycled Western-medieval template.

### Playable imagination

Even though the world is unusual, it should remain playable, understandable, and rich in possibilities for stories, campaigns, and characters.
